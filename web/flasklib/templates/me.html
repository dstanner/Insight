Darren Tanner , Ph.D.
I received my doctoral degree in the Department of Linguistics with a neuroscience focus from the University of Washington. I then spent two years as a postdoctoral researcher in the Center for Language Science in the Department of Psychology at Penn State University. I followed this up with five years on the faculty of Linguistics at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where I had joint appointments in the departments of Pyschology and Spanish and Portuguese, as well as affiliations with the Neuroscience Program, the Beckman Institute for Advanced Science and Technology, and the Second Language Acquisition and Teacher Education program.

My academic research focused on the cognitive and neural underpinnings of language use (mostly language comprehension), second language learning in adulthood, and multilingualism. I mainly used recordings of electrical brain activity (electroencephalography) to understand the dynamics of how language processes unfold in the brain over time during real-time language use. I completemented this neuroscientific research with eye tracking and reaction-time research (including large-scale, web-based data collection). I'm known in the cognitive science and psycholinguistics communities primarily for my work on individual differences in the neural antecedents of language processing.

You can read more about my academic work and see my CV at my academic wordpress site.
